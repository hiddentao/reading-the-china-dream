_Rethinking China's Rise: A Liberal Critique_, is a translation of eight recent essays by **Xu Jilin**, professor of history at East China Normal University in Shanghai, and an important public intellectual. The essays have been translated and edited by **David Ownby**, and the volume was published by Cambridge University Press in July of 2018\. Click [here](http://www.cambridge.org/9781108470759) to order. To see reviews of the volume, click [here](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/709003), [here](https://search.proquest.com/docview/2293661598?pq-origsite=gscholar&fromopenview=true), and [here](https://www.pressreader.com/usa/the-new-york-review-of-books/20200326/281582357668300).  
  
The volume includes:  
  
David Ownby, "[Editor and Translator's Introduction](/ownby-introduction-to-xu-jilin/)"  
  
Xu Jilin, "[What Kind of Civilization? China at a Crossroads](/xu-jilin-what-kind-of-civilization/)"  
  
Xu Jilin, "The Specter of Leviathan: A Critique of Chinese Statism since 2000"  
  
Xu Jilin, "Universal Civilization, or Chinese Values? A Critique of Historicist Thought since 2000"  
  
Xu Jilin, "[After the 'Great Disembedding': Family-State, _Tianxia_, and Self](/xu-jilin-great-disembedding/)"  
  
Xu Jilin, "[What Body for Confucianism's Lonely Soul](/xu-jilin-what-body-for-confucianism/)"  
  
Xu Jilin, "[The New _Tianxia_: Rebuilding China's Internal and External Order](/xu-jilin-the-new-tianxia/)"  
  
Xu Jilin, "Two Kinds of Enlightenment: Civilizational Consciousness or Cultural Consciousness  
  
Xu Jilin, "Li Shenzhi: The Last Scholar-Official, the Last Hero"
